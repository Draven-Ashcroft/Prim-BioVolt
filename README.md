# ⚡ Prim-BioVolt

> An interactive simulation of the Miller–Urey Experiment (1953) — exploring the prebiotic synthesis of amino acids on early Earth.

## 🧪 What is this?

**Prim-BioVolt** simulates Stanley L. Miller and Harold C. Urey's landmark 1953 experiment at the University of Chicago, which demonstrated that organic molecules — the building blocks of life — could form spontaneously from inorganic gases under early Earth conditions.

This simulation was built as an **interactive educational tool** for Class 10 Biology students.

---

## ✨ Features

- 🔬 **Animated apparatus** — boiling flask, spark chamber, condenser, and U-trap rendered on HTML5 Canvas
- ⚡ **Electric spark simulation** — trigger manually or run in auto mode
- 🔥 **Bunsen burner toggle** — controls heating and particle flow speed
- 🌡️ **Live temperature gauges** — Boiling Flask, Condenser, and Spark Chamber
- 📈 **Oscilloscope waveform** — visualises discharge pulses in real time
- 🧬 **Amino acid detection** — compounds appear as particles accumulate in the U-trap
- 📊 **Run statistics** — tracks amino acids formed, sparks fired, water cycles, and simulation time
- 📅 **Experiment timeline** — 7-day progress bar
- 🔍 **Molecule inspector** — click any detected compound to view its structure, properties, and facts
- 🏷️ **Callout card labels** — clean annotated overlays for each apparatus component

## 📚 Scientific Background

The Miller–Urey experiment tested the **Oparin–Haldane hypothesis** — that the conditions of early Earth could spontaneously produce organic molecules.

**Setup:**
- A sealed system containing H₂O, CH₄, NH₃, and H₂
- A boiling flask simulating the primordial ocean
- Electric sparks simulating lightning
- A condenser cooling vapour back to liquid
- A U-trap collecting organic compounds

**Result:** After one week, ~2% of the carbon had formed amino acids — proof that the chemistry of life can arise abiotically.
